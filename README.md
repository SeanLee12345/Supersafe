# About This Project

## Decentralized and AI-Assisted Media Verification

AI-generated and manipulated images and videos are becoming increasingly difficult to detect. As synthetic media becomes more realistic, it is harder for ordinary viewers to know whether a piece of media is authentic, altered, or presented in a misleading way.

This project explores a hybrid approach to media verification by combining AI-assisted analysis, human review, and decentralized dispute resolution through UMA. Instead of relying only on an AI detector or simple majority voting, the goal is to create a system where media can be reviewed through multiple forms of evidence and, when necessary, escalated through an incentive-based verification process.

The project focuses on the question:

> Has this image or video been materially altered, generated, or presented in a way that could change its meaning or mislead a reasonable viewer?

A “material alteration” does not mean every small edit. Basic cropping, compression, resizing, or color correction may not change the meaning of a piece of media. The focus is on edits that affect how someone understands the people, objects, actions, setting, timing, or context shown in the media.

## What the Platform Does

The platform is intended to let users submit media for review, view available evidence, and evaluate whether the media appears authentic or misleading. Depending on the final implementation, the system may include AI detection outputs, metadata analysis, human labels, and UMA-based claim or dispute resolution.

A basic workflow could include:

1. A user uploads or submits an image or video.
2. The system displays relevant evidence, such as metadata, AI analysis, or user-provided context.
3. Reviewers evaluate whether the media appears materially altered or misleading.
4. A claim can be submitted through UMA’s oracle system.
5. If the claim is challenged, UMA’s dispute-resolution process can help determine the outcome.
6. The platform displays the final result with supporting evidence and limitations.

## Why UMA Matters

UMA provides a decentralized oracle and dispute-resolution framework. In this project, UMA is used to explore how disputed claims about media authenticity could be handled through an incentive-based system rather than relying only on a centralized moderator, AI model, or public vote.

This does not mean UMA automatically determines whether media is real or fake. Instead, UMA can serve as a mechanism for resolving claims when there is disagreement. The project uses UMA to test whether decentralized verification can improve trust, transparency, and accountability in media review.

## Project Goals

The main goals of this project are to:

- Build a prototype for media verification.
- Explore the limits of AI-based media detection.
- Define what counts as a meaningful or material alteration.
- Include real UMA integration as part of the verification workflow.
- Compare centralized, human, AI-assisted, and decentralized approaches.
- Document the strengths, weaknesses, and future potential of the system.

## Limitations

This project is a prototype and should not be treated as a production-level truth verification system. AI detectors can be wrong, human reviewers can be biased or misled, and some media claims may be too subjective to resolve with certainty. The project is designed to explore these challenges rather than claim to fully solve them.

## Future Direction

Future versions could support stronger AI models, better provenance tracking, video analysis, browser extensions, social media integrations, expert review, and expanded UMA dispute workflows. The long-term goal would be a media integrity system that helps users evaluate digital content with more transparency and accountability.
